About Britt

Britt Parramore is the Clinical Director of Pathlight Counseling and one of the driving forces behind what the practice has become since 2017. As a Licensed Professional Counselor (LPC) and Certified Advanced Alcohol and Drug Counselor (CAADC), he brings both deep clinical training and a personal understanding of recovery that changes the way he shows up for every client.

With more than 11 years in the field — and currently working toward his doctorate — Britt has built a practice that refuses to separate clinical excellence from genuine human connection. He believes that lasting recovery requires treating the whole person: the habits, the history, the relationships, the beliefs, and the parts of yourself you've been too afraid to look at.

A Clinician Who Understands from the Inside

One of the things that sets Britt apart is something you won't find on most therapist bios: he is in personal recovery himself. That's not a liability — it's one of the most powerful things he brings into the room. When Britt tells a client that recovery is possible, it's not a clinical statement. It's lived testimony.

Clients who have struggled with addiction often describe feeling misunderstood by therapists who treat their problem as a behavior to correct rather than a person to understand. With Britt, that gap closes quickly.

Specialized Focus on Veterans

Britt has developed particular expertise working with veterans and active-duty service members. He understands the unique pressures of military culture, the specific ways trauma manifests after service, and the barriers veterans face when seeking help. Through Pathlight's fully grant-funded veteran services program, Britt and the team are able to offer this care completely free to veterans and their families.

Faith and Clinical Practice

Britt integrates Christian principles into his clinical work — not as a substitute for evidence-based care, but as a complement to it. For clients whose faith is part of who they are, that integration often makes the difference between therapy that feels generic and therapy that actually fits. And for clients who prefer a strictly secular approach, he meets them there too.
