What you're feeling has a name, and a treatment

Depression isn't a character flaw. And it's not something you should just push through.

Depression has a way of convincing you that this is just how things are. That you're lazy, or ungrateful, or weak for not being able to feel better. None of that is true. Depression is a real condition that changes how you think, feel, and function, and it responds to treatment.

At Pathlight Counseling in Woodstock, GA, we work with adults and teens who are tired of waking up already dreading the day. Our counselors combine evidence-based therapy with genuine human connection, because both matter in treating depression.

You don't have to keep feeling this way. The first step is a free, confidential conversation.

What depression can look like

Depression shows up differently for everyone

→Persistent sadness, emptiness, or hopelessness that doesn't lift
→Loss of interest in things you used to enjoy, hobbies, people, activities
→Fatigue and low energy even when you've slept
→Difficulty concentrating, making decisions, or remembering things
→Changes in appetite or sleep that are hard to explain
→A sense of just going through the motions, present in your life but not really living it

You deserve to feel better. That's not too much to ask.
