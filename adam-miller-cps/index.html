About Adam

Adam E. Miller is a Certified Peer Specialist in Addiction (CPS-AD) at Pathlight Counseling in Woodstock, GA. His additional certifications in crisis intervention (CIT) and addiction recovery support (CARES) reflect a skill set built around meeting people in their most vulnerable moments and helping them take the next step forward.

What makes Adam different is where his work starts: not in a textbook, but in his own story. Adam is in personal recovery himself, and he brings that lived experience directly into his work with clients. He's not someone who has studied addiction from a distance — he knows what it actually feels like to be in it, and what it actually takes to get out.

What Peer Support Really Means

There's something uniquely powerful about being supported by someone who has walked the same road. Professional training matters — but so does the credibility that only comes from personal experience. When Adam tells a client that recovery is possible, that statement lands differently than it does coming from someone who's only observed it.

Peer support is not a lesser form of care — it's a distinct and often essential component of lasting recovery. Research consistently shows that peer support improves engagement in treatment, reduces relapse rates, and helps people rebuild the sense of connection that addiction typically destroys.

Crisis Intervention & Stabilization

Adam's CIT certification means he's trained to work with individuals in acute crisis — moments when the situation is escalating and the right response matters enormously. He helps stabilize those situations and connect people to the right level of care, without judgment and without making things worse.

Whether you're in crisis right now or somewhere on the longer road of recovery, Adam's presence on the Pathlight team means there's someone who genuinely understands where you are.
