About Dr. Carpenter

Dr. Michael Reece Carpenter brings a rare combination of doctoral-level clinical training and decades of hands-on prevention work to his role at Pathlight Counseling. As an EMDR-trained therapist, he works with clients whose addiction and mental health challenges are rooted in past trauma, helping them process what has been stuck for years and move forward with their lives.

Dr. Carpenter holds a Bachelor of Science from the University of Tennessee, a Master's Degree in Community Counseling from Memphis State University, and a Doctorate of Philosophy from Georgia State University. His academic background, combined with years of field experience across Georgia's public and private education systems, gives him a uniquely practical understanding of how trauma, addiction, and mental health intersect.

A Career Built on Prevention and Healing

Before joining Pathlight Counseling, Dr. Carpenter spent years working as a prevention and intervention specialist across multiple Georgia counties, including Cobb, Fayette, Cherokee, and Lumpkin. He worked statewide for the Departments of Education and Human Resources and has served in international consulting roles. His focus has always been on stopping harm before it takes root, and healing it when it already has.

He is a National Bullying Prevention and Student Assistance Trainer, a certified QPR and CALM trainer in suicide prevention, and a Georgia Certified Prevention Specialist. He has authored state grants for DBHDD and Drug-Free Community initiatives, and his published works include three books on bullying prevention and school safety.

EMDR: Trauma Treatment That Actually Works

Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR) is one of the most well-researched and effective approaches to trauma treatment available today. Dr. Carpenter uses EMDR to help clients at Pathlight process difficult memories and experiences that continue to drive anxiety, addiction, and emotional pain, even when talk therapy alone hasn't been enough.

For clients dealing with addiction that is rooted in unprocessed trauma, this approach can be genuinely life-changing. Dr. Carpenter works alongside the rest of the Pathlight team to integrate trauma-focused care into the full treatment picture.
