Common Questions

What people ask before calling.

If something in your life isn't working — anxiety that won't quit, a sadness you can't shake, a pattern you keep repeating — those are exactly the things therapy is designed for. You don't have to be in crisis to benefit. Many people start therapy simply because they want to feel better than they do right now, and that's enough of a reason. The first step is just a conversation — low-pressure, no commitment required.

The first session is really just a conversation. Your therapist will ask about what brought you in, a bit about your background, and what you're hoping to change or work through. There's nothing you need to prepare. You won't be put on the spot or handed a stack of forms. The goal is simply for both of you to get a sense of each other and figure out the best path forward.

It depends on what you're working on. Some people see meaningful progress in 8–12 sessions. Others work with a therapist for a year or more on deeper or more complex issues. We won't keep you in therapy longer than is helpful — and we'll check in regularly to make sure you're getting what you came for. The goal is always your growth, not a never-ending appointment schedule.

We accept Humana, Anthem BC/BS, Blue Cross/Blue Shield, Oxford, Cigna, United Healthcare, and out-of-network plans. Call us at (470) 249-4499 and we'll verify your benefits before your first appointment — so you know exactly what to expect financially before you commit to anything. We believe cost shouldn't be the reason someone doesn't get help.

We ask for 24 hours' notice if you need to cancel or reschedule. Life happens, and we try to be understanding — but last-minute cancellations do impact other clients who may need that appointment time. Your therapist will walk you through the specifics during your first session so there are no surprises.

Yes. We offer telehealth sessions for clients who can't come in person or simply prefer to meet from home. Many clients find telehealth works just as well as in-person — especially once you've already established a relationship with your therapist. Ask about availability when you schedule your first consultation.

Our office is located in Woodstock, GA — right in the heart of Cherokee County. We regularly see clients from Canton, Holly Springs, Kennesaw, Acworth, Marietta, and surrounding areas. Our Intensive Outpatient Programs draw families from across North Georgia, including Cobb, Forsyth, Pickens, Dawson, and North Fulton counties.

A few things set us apart. We offer the only Teen Intensive Outpatient Program in Cherokee County — built specifically for adolescents, not adapted from an adult model. Many of our clinicians are in personal recovery themselves, which means they bring lived experience alongside their credentials. We also provide fully free veteran services through grant funding. And for clients whose faith matters to them, we integrate clinical and Christian principles — if and how you want that.

Therapeutic fit matters more than most people realize — and we take it seriously. If you work with a therapist and something doesn't feel right, say so. We'd much rather find you a better match within our team than have you stop coming altogether. The relationship between you and your therapist is part of what makes therapy work, and we want to get that right.

Yes. Everything shared in counseling is protected by strict confidentiality laws. The only exceptions are narrow legal requirements — like if someone is at immediate risk of harm, or if there is a legal mandate. We'll explain all of this clearly before your first session so you know exactly where the lines are. Most clients find that knowing they're protected makes it much easier to open up.
