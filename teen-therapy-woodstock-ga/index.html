For parents who know something isn't right

You've watched your teenager struggle. Now it's time to get them real support.

Teenagers aren't always able, or willing, to tell you what's going on. What you see instead is the behavior: the withdrawal, the irritability, the grades slipping, the friendships changing, the sleep problems that never existed before. You know something is wrong. You're just not sure what to do about it.

At Pathlight Counseling in Woodstock, GA, we work with adolescents ages 13–17 who are dealing with anxiety, depression, substance use, trauma, and the ordinary-but-hard parts of growing up. Our counselors know how to build trust with teenagers, which makes all the difference in whether therapy actually helps.

We also offer the only Teen Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP) in Cherokee County, a 4-month ASAM Level 2.1 program purpose-built for adolescents, not adapted from an adult model.

What brings teens to Pathlight

Signs your teenager might benefit from therapy

→Anxiety or worry that's affecting their school performance or social life
→Depression, persistent sadness, or withdrawal from activities they used to enjoy
→Substance use, experimentation that's escalating, or dependency that's developing
→Trauma from bullying, family disruption, loss, or past experiences
→Explosive anger, defiance, or emotional regulation challenges
→A gut feeling as a parent that something is wrong, even if you can't name it

The sooner you reach out, the easier it is to turn around.

A free, confidential parent consultation is the first step. We'll listen to what you're seeing, answer your questions, and help you figure out the right level of support for your teenager.
