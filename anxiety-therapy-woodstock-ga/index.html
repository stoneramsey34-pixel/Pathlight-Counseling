You don't have to keep white-knuckling it

Anxiety is exhausting. And you don't have to manage it alone.

Anxiety isn't just worry — it's the kind of mental and physical exhaustion that comes from being on high alert all the time. The tension in your shoulders. The sleep that doesn't come. The dread of things that haven't happened yet. Over time, anxiety starts to shrink your world.

At Pathlight Counseling in Woodstock, GA, we work with adults and teens who are ready to stop letting anxiety run the show. Our counselors use evidence-based approaches — including Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) — to help you understand what's driving your anxiety and change the patterns that keep it going.

The first step is a free, confidential conversation. No commitment. Just a chance to tell us what's going on and figure out whether we're the right fit.

Signs anxiety therapy might help

Does this sound familiar?

→You can't stop worrying, even when things are objectively fine
→You avoid situations or places because of fear or dread
→Anxiety is affecting your sleep, your work, or your relationships
→You experience panic attacks or sudden waves of fear
→Physical symptoms — tight chest, upset stomach, headaches — with no clear medical cause
→You feel like you can never fully relax, even when nothing is wrong

Our approach

How we treat anxiety at Pathlight

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT is one of the most research-supported treatments for anxiety. It helps you identify the thoughts and patterns that feed your anxiety — and change them.

Whole-person care

We look at what's driving your anxiety beneath the surface — past experiences, relationships, and the stories you've been telling yourself for years.

Faith-integrated if you want it

For clients whose faith matters to them, we can integrate Christian principles meaningfully alongside evidence-based care.
