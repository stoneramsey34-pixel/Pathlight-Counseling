About Stone

Stone is a dedicated Marriage and Family Therapist and current Doctoral Candidate at Lee University, where he is pursuing his Ph.D. in Marriage and Family Therapy. He brings a wealth of clinical experience from his work at Tri-State Therapeutic Riding Center, the Family Therapy Center at Lee University, and various faith-based youth programs.

As a full-time therapist at Pathlight Counseling, Stone provides compassionate care through both individual sessions and the Intensive Outpatient Program. He works with couples, families, and individuals navigating a wide range of challenges — from addiction and anxiety to relational wounds that have never had a chance to heal.

Specialized Focus on Compulsive Sexual Behavior

Stone's area of specialized doctoral study is compulsive and unwanted sexual behavior — one of the most misunderstood and under-treated struggles people bring into therapy. He works with individuals and couples navigating the impact of pornography use, sexual compulsivity, infidelity, and the betrayal trauma that often follows.

This isn't an area many therapists in North Georgia specialize in. For clients who have felt ashamed to name what's really going on, working with Stone offers a rare combination: clinical expertise, genuine understanding, and an environment completely free of judgment.

Grounded in Faith and Family

Stone's approach to therapy is grounded in his faith and his deep commitment to strengthening marriages and families. He is passionate about helping parents navigate the unique challenges of raising children in today's digital landscape — including conversations about pornography, screen time, and online relationships that most families aren't sure how to have.

He is an active member of Restoration Church in Alpharetta and serves as a board member for Georgians for Responsible Marijuana Policy. Outside the office, Stone stays active through exercise and enjoys hiking, hunting, and fishing in the North Georgia mountains.
