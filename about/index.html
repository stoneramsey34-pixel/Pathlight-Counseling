Our story

Why we do this work

Pathlight Counseling opened in 2017 with one conviction: that people struggling with addiction and mental health challenges deserve care that treats them as a whole person — not a case number, not a diagnosis, and not a lost cause.

Many members of our team are in personal recovery themselves. This isn't incidental — it's foundational to how we work. They bring the clinical training and credentials you'd expect, and they bring something no degree program can give: they know what it actually feels like to be where you are right now. That perspective shapes every session, every conversation, every relationship we have with clients.

We bring together clinically sound approaches with biblically grounded principles — because for many people in recovery, faith is part of the foundation they're rebuilding on. If that's not your path, that's okay too. We meet you where you are.

What to expect

What working with Pathlight looks like

Step 1: A real conversation

Your first call is free, confidential, and there's no intake form to fill out. You just talk. We listen. No pressure, no commitment.

Step 2: We figure out what fits

We'll talk honestly about what kind of support — individual counseling, IOP, or a veterans program — makes the most sense given where you are right now.

Step 3: You start building

Recovery isn't a straight line, and we'll be here for all of it — not just the easy parts. You're not starting over. You're starting from here.

I'd love to hear from you.

Your story isn't over. The next chapter can look completely different from where you are right now. We've seen it happen — and we'd be honored to be part of yours.
