You served. Now let us serve you.

What you went through matters. So does how you're living now.

A lot of veterans tell us they thought they were fine. They got back, they got back to life — to their family, their job, some version of a routine. And then slowly, or suddenly, it became clear that something wasn't right.

Sleeplessness. Anger that comes out sideways. Numbing with alcohol or other substances. Distance from people you love. Replaying things you'd rather not replay. These aren't signs of weakness. They're signs of what you went through.

At Pathlight, we work with veterans and active-duty service members who are ready to address what they've been carrying — whether that's trauma, substance use, depression, or simply the difficulty of coming home to a civilian life that doesn't always understand what you've been through.

You might recognize this

Experiences that bring veterans to Pathlight

→Memories or images from service that intrude when you don't want them to
→Alcohol or substance use that escalated after returning home
→Difficulty connecting with family, or feeling like they can't understand what you've been through
→Hypervigilance — always scanning, always alert — even when you're safe
→Depression or emptiness that's hard to explain or shake
→Trouble with the transition to civilian life — the pace, the purpose, the identity shift

You don't have to keep carrying this alone.

The first consultation is free, confidential, and there's no commitment. Just a conversation with someone who takes what you've been through seriously.

How we can help

Support designed with your service in mind

Free to veterans & all family members

Our veterans services are fully grant-funded through COSSUP. There is no cost to you or your family — no insurance needed, no out-of-pocket expenses, no financial barriers.

Forsyth County RSAT Program

We operate the Forsyth County RSAT Program, providing substance abuse treatment within the criminal justice system — including for veterans navigating the justice system.

Trauma-informed care

Our counselors understand trauma — what it does to the brain and body, and what it takes to actually process it rather than just manage it.

Substance use and mental health together

For many veterans, substance use and mental health are connected. We treat both — because treating one while ignoring the other rarely produces lasting change.

Faith integration available

For veterans whose faith is part of their foundation, we can incorporate that meaningfully into the work. Many veterans find that faith and recovery support each other.

Support for families too

The people closest to veterans often need support too. We work with families to help them understand what their veteran is going through and how to be part of the healing.
